Test-time Adaptation 6D Pose Tracking for Unseen Objects

Long Tian, Andrea Cavallaro, Changjae Oh

Centre for Intelligent Sensing, Queen Mary University of London, United Kingdom

ABSTRACT: We present a test-time adaptation strategy that effectively transfers knowledge from known objects to unseen objects in the domain of 6D pose tracking. Our approach addresses object pose tracking as a 3D keypoint detection and matching task. By leveraging RGB-D data and the target object mask as inputs, we extract point-wise features that encode both appearance and geometric information. To enhance feature discrimination, we employ self-attention, enabling points to communicate within frames, and cross-attention, facilitating interaction between points across frames. Keypoints are then detected from the enhanced features. Pose changes are subsequently recovered through a least-squares optimisation using keypoint sets from two time instants. Initially, our proposed network is trained in a supervised manner on a source domain, acquiring prior knowledge of 6D pose tracking at a category-level. Subsequently, the trained model parameters are updated to adapt to unseen objects. To avoid reliance on ground truth pose changes, we introduce two self-supervised losses based on photometric and geometric consistency. Extensive experiments are conducted on the NOCS-REAL275 dataset and our collected dataset.



The proposed framework for keypoints detection. It begins by extracting the fused appearance and geometric features of the target object from the cropped RGB and the target point cloud. Subsequently, we utilise self-attention and cross-attention to enhance points features and obtain more distinguished intra-frame and inter-frame features. Following, we combine points features into one global feature using a multilayer perceptron (MLP) layer and the 3D keypoints are estimated through a fully connected (FC) layer.
