Cluster-based Unsupervised 3D Keypoints Prediction for Category-agnostic 6D Object Pose Tracking

Long Tian, Andrea Cavallaro, Changjae Oh

Centre for Intelligent Sensing, Queen Mary University of London, United Kingdom

We present a novel deep learning approach for category-agnostic 6D object pose tracking. Unlike category-specific approaches, our method requires no CAD models or annotated keypoints of the objects to track their pose. We tackle 6D object pose tracking as an unsupervised 3D keypoint prediction and matching. We present the target object as 3D point clouds and separate these points into clusters to estimate the 3D keypoint from each cluster, which improves the efficiency of keypoint prediction. The inter-frame change of the 6D pose can be obtained by matching keypoints between the previous and current frame, which enables 6D pose tracking. Our cluster-based keypoint prediction does not require prior knowledge of the object categories, which enables category-agnostic 6D pose tracking. Experiments on the NOCS-REAL275 dataset show that the proposed method is competitive to the existing category-specific methods.
